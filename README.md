This repository contains my complete submission for CSE0001 – Digital Literacy, a foundational course offered to all first-year B.Tech students at VIT Bhopal University through the VITyarthi E-Learning Platform. The course carries 1 credit and is evaluated on a Pass/Fail basis, meaning it does not affect CGPA but forms a permanent part of my academic record.
The central premise of this project is a real-world scenario: I have been selected as a Student Digital Ambassador for my batch. In this role, my responsibility is to help fellow students navigate the digital world — from building a professional online presence, to communicating responsibly, to staying safe from online threats. This portfolio is what I would present to the faculty committee as proof of that readiness.
The project is divided into five tasks, each mapping directly to one module of the course:
•	Task 1 focuses on visual communication — I created a one-page digital literacy infographic using Canva to help my batchmates understand what digital literacy means and why it matters in today's world.
•	Task 2 is about professional presence — I set up accounts on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle, platforms that employers, internship coordinators, and the academic community actively look at.
•	Task 3 builds familiarity with tools for learning and collaboration — I completed a beginner coding challenge on HackerRank and created a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms, which could genuinely be used to quiz my batchmates.
•	Task 4 covers professional communication — I drafted two formal emails (one to a professor, one to an internship coordinator) and created a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist for college students.
•	Task 5 addresses online safety — I researched UPI and online payment fraud, wrote a detailed case study, and produced a prevention checklist tailored to Indian college students, including the national cyber crime helpline (1930) and portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
All five tasks are documented in this repository with supporting screenshots, files, and notes organised into clearly labelled folders. The full written report, covering reflections and observations for each task, is available in the report/ folder.
Every tool used in this project — Canva, GitHub, Google Forms, HackerRank — is free and entirely browser-based. No installation or prior coding experience was required, which reflects the course's intent: to make digital literacy accessible to every student, regardless of technical background.

Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
Module 1 | 20 Marks
I created a one-page digital literacy infographic using Canva, titled ‘Navigating the Digital World: A Student’s Guide.’ The infographic covers three core topics:
•	What Digital Literacy means for today’s students
•	Useful digital tools for academic and professional use
•	Safe internet practices every student should follow
The design uses a clean blue-and-white colour scheme with icons to make content visually scannable. The exported image is in the task-1-presentation/ folder.
Tool used: Canva (canva.com) — free, browser-based, no installation required.

Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio
Module 2 | 20 Marks
I set up accounts on three key professional platforms to build my foundational digital presence:
•	GitHub — Created a profile README with my name, branch, year, and learning goals. Also created this project repository.
•	LinkedIn — Filled in the Education section with my B.Tech programme, institution (VIT Bhopal University), and expected graduation year.
•	Kaggle — Set up a profile to begin exploring data science challenges and datasets in future semesters.
Screenshots of all three profiles are in the task-2-portfolio/ folder. Only college email was used for sign-ups; all profiles are professional and minimal.

 
Task 3 – Coding and Collaboration Platforms
Module 3 | 20 Marks

Created an account on HackerRank and completed the ‘Solve Me First’ beginner challenge (sum of two integers). Screenshot of the completed challenge and badge is in task-3-platforms/.

Created a Google Form titled ‘Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz’ with exactly 5 questions for batchmates, including:

Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study and Prevention Guide
Module 5 | 20 Marks
Part A – Case Study (UPI / Online Payment Fraud):
The case study describes how fraudsters impersonate bank officials or e-commerce sellers to trick victims into sharing UPI PINs or OTPs. It covers: what the crime is, how it unfolds step-by-step, who is typically targeted (students and first-time UPI users), and the consequences (financial loss, difficulty of recovery). File: casestudy.md.
Part B – Stay Safe Online Prevention Checklist:
An 8-point checklist of specific, actionable tips for college students in India. Includes at least 2 UPI/financial safety tips and the official reporting channels:
•	National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
•	Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (24x7, Government of India)
File: prevention-checklist.md in task-5-cybercrime/.

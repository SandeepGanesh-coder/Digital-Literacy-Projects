✅ The Goal Oriented (Do’s)
•	Keep it kind: It is like “Take care of the pence  , and the pounds will take care of themselves” Treat every comment like a face-to-face conversation. 
                 A little politeness goes a long way.
•	Check the facts:  Always think of “Going The whole nine yards”Before you hit share, take a second to make sure it’s actually true. Real news doesn’t need a rush.
•	Lock your doors: As the saying goes “Better safe than sorry” Regularly check your privacy settings. Your personal business belongs to you, not the whole world.
•	Think of "Future You":  In the world of technology they say “failing to plan is planning to fail”Ask yourself if your boss or your future self will
                          be proud of this post five years from now.
•	Find your tribe:  Always be in the search of “charting unknown  waters”. We should always be in the urge of learning and implementing new things.
                    Use your feed to learn new things, meet cool people, and spread a bit of positivity.
❌ The "Maybe Not" (Don’ts)
•	Keep secrets secret: In the field of technology it is always thought of “Don’t let the cat out of the bag” Never post sensitive details like your home address,
                       bank info, or private passwords.
•	Skip the drama:  Always think of the famous lines “The dogs bark , but the caravan moves on” Steer clear of posts that are mean-spirited, offensive,
                   or just plain messy.
•	Don’t feed the trolls: It is said by wise men that  “Fire cannot be fought with fire ; it is put out with water”.Walking away from an online argument
                         isn't losing—it’s protecting your peace.
•	Less is more: ”Empty vessels make the most noise ”  , the true essence of saying less contents to signify more information .
                 You don't have to broadcast your exact location or every detail of your day in real-time.
•	Look beyond the headline: We should not get swayed by  the headings , we should focus on the content too ,
as in the words of William Blake “A Fool sees not the same tree that a wise man sees”  
Social media is great for updates, but always double-check a story with a trusted source before believing it.
